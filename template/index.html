Helen Keller

American author, political activist, and lecturer

Here's a time line of Helen's life:

  • 1880 - Born on June 27, 1880, in Tuscumbia, Alabama.
  • 1882 - Contracts an unknown illness, at 19 months old, that leaves her both deaf and blind.
  • 1887 - Meets Sullivan and learns her firts word, "d-o-l-l". "The mystery of language is revealed to me" --Helen.
  • 1888 - Attends the Perkins Institute for the Blind.
  • 1894 - Moves to New York, with Sullivan, to attend the Wright-Humason School for the Deaf.
  • 1896 - Returs to Massachusetts, and enters The Cambridge School for Young Ladies.
  • 1900 - Enters Radcliffe College of Harvard. University.
  • 1903 - Publishes her first book, an autobiography, The Story of My Life
  • 1904 - Graduats from Harvard University. First deaf-blind person to earn a Bachelor of Arts degree.
  • 1909 - Becomes a political activist. Actively campaigns and writes in support of the working class for over a decade
  • 1915 - Establishes the Helen Keller International (HKI) organization with George A. Kessler. This organization is devoted to research in vision, health and nutrition.
  • 1946 - Visits 35 countries in the next decade to support blind and deaf awareness.
  • 1964 - Receives the Presidential Medal of Freedom, one of the United States' two highest civilian honors.
  • 1968 - Dies at the age of 87.

"The most important day I remember in all my life is the one on which my teacher, Anne Mansfield Sullivan, came to me. I am filled with wonder when I consider the immeasurable contrast between the two lives which it connects." -- Helen Keller
